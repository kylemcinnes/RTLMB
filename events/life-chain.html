Life Chain (Canada)

An annual, peaceful, prayerful public witness for children killed by abortion—held in ~350 locations across Canada. The next Life Chain is Sunday, October 4, 2026.

Find your Life Chain

What to expect (1 hour)

Stand in silent prayer with a sign provided by local organizers; families welcome. This is a peaceful, prayerful witness that brings communities together to remember the children lost to abortion.

  • One-hour commitment
  • Signs provided by organizers
  • Family-friendly event
  • Silent prayer and reflection

When is the next Life Chain?

Sunday, October 4, 2026 (2:00–3:00 pm in most locations). Some communities hold Life Chain at different times—always verify your city's listing on the official Life Chain Canada website. The 2026 location list will be posted in August.

Mississauga & Brampton

Peel Region hosts Life Chain at many parish-led locations. Times vary by site—confirm details on lifechain.ca once the 2026 list is published (expected August 2026). Recent locations have included:

Mississauga examples: Sts. Peter & Paul (Central Pkwy E at Burnhamthorpe Rd E, 2–3 pm), St. Martin of Tours (Dundas St W near Erindale, 2–3 pm), St. Maximilian Kolbe (Cawthra Rd at Meadows Blvd, 2–3 pm), and others across the city.

Brampton examples: St. John Fisher (300 Balmoral Dr, 1:30–2:30 pm), St. Marguerite d'Youville (Sandalwood Pkwy E at Torbram Rd, 2–3 pm), St. Anne's (115 Vodden St E, 1:15–2:15 pm), and others.

How to participate

Use the Life Chain Canada website to find your location, time, and local organizer details; bring water and weather-appropriate clothing; arrive 10 minutes early.

  • Find your local organizer at lifechain.ca
  • Dress for the weather
  • Bring water and arrive early
  • Follow the Life Chain Code of Conduct

About Life Chain Canada

Life Chain Canada coordinates this annual public witness across the country, supported by Campaign Life Coalition and local right-to-life groups.
